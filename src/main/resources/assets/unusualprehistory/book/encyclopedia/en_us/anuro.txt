Oddities in the pterosaur families for their flat faces and forward-facing eyes, Anurognathus look more akin to a frog-owl
than any other pterosaur. These pterosaurs are primarily insectivores, consuming arthropods quite voraciously. A curious
behavior sighted in these animals is pollination similar to that of bees, growing crops much faster.
